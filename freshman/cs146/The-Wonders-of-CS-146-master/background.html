History of Computation

The abacus was pretty cool, because you could be dumb and still do some math without messing it up.

Joseph Marie Jacquard was the dude who made punchards a thing, specifically for making a programmable loom.

In comes Charles Babbage, the father of modern computing, with his difference engines #1 & #2.

George Boole is who you can thank for having to memorize truth tables for your exams.

Howard Aiken was a nerd who worked at Big Blue who made some computers that helped us become world war champs twice over.

Apple swooped in with their Apple 1 machine, which was one of the first personal computers.

Big Blue popularized home computing using parts manufactured by other companies in their IBM PC, making windows a big deal

History of Communication

Communication started with cave paintings, which most everyone has seen before in 2nd grade. Then came writing, which was created way back around 4000 BC. Eventually around 0 AD the Chinese made paper which spread into Western Cultures.

The printing revolution came a little while later, making it easier to produce a lot of print for newspapers.

In 1843 Samuel Finley Breese Morse built a telegraph system from Washington D.C. to Baltimore. He even created his own language to go along with it! (Morse code)

From here things pick up pretty quickly, and somehow we go from a two way telephone in 1876, to the telex in 1933, to the fax machine in 1970 and finally to the internet (Wide Area Network) in 1985.
